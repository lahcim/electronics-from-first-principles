# Volume 3 Challenge Answer Key

1. 25 µs period corresponds to 40 kHz.
2. A waveform spanning 0.2–3.0 V has 1.6 V offset and 1.4 V peak amplitude.
3. 30° at 5 kHz corresponds to 16.67 µs.
4. A 2 V peak half-wave-rectified sine averages $V_P/\pi\approx0.637$ V.
5. 120 V RMS sine has approximately 169.7 V peak.
6. 12 V at 25% duty has 6 V RMS.
7. Resistor power remains nonnegative because voltage and current reverse together.
8. $3+j4=5\angle53.13^\circ$.
9. Equal opposite phasors cancel to zero.
10. Real resistors acquire parasitic inductance and capacitance.
11. 1 µF has 1 kΩ reactance near 159.15 Hz.
12. 10 mH has 1 kΩ reactance near 15.915 kHz.
13. At the RC corner, magnitude is $R\sqrt2$ and phase −45° for total impedance.
14. Add conductances and susceptances before converting back to magnitude and phase.
15. For 100 Ω and 10 mH, the corner is about 1.5915 kHz.
16. Set $2\pi fL=R$; for 1 kΩ at 2 kHz, L ≈ 79.6 mH.
17. Use sweep limits at least one decade below the lowest corner and one decade above the highest.
18. Gain 0.1 = −20 dB; 0.5 = −6.02 dB; 2 = +6.02 dB; 100 = +40 dB.
19. 10 kΩ and 10 nF give a pole near 1.5915 kHz.
20. A 500 Hz low-pass can use 3.3 kΩ and 100 nF for about 482 Hz, or another nearby standard pair.
21. With 100 kΩ input and 20 Hz target, C ≈ 79.6 nF; 82 nF is a practical value.
22. For 100 Ω at 1 kHz, L ≈ 15.9 mH.
23. Buffering or at least 10:1 impedance separation reduces passive-stage loading.
24. Include the 2 kΩ source resistance in the total R used for the RC corner.
25. To keep divider-like passband loss below 1%, load resistance should be roughly 100× the source/filter resistance.
26. Audio line interfaces usually maximize voltage transfer with high input impedance rather than maximum-power matching.
27. A 300 Hz–3 kHz passive band-pass needs high-pass and low-pass corners plus loading analysis; buffering gives predictable response.
28. Any L/C pair satisfying $LC=1/(2\pi10\text{kHz})^2$ is valid.
29. Large L and C voltages are opposite in phase at resonance and cancel in the KVL sum.
30. Real parallel resonance is limited by DCR, ESR, and core loss.
31. For Q = 5, L = 10 mH, and 1 kHz, R ≈ 12.57 Ω.
32. Lower damping gives more overshoot and ringing; critical damping is the fastest non-overshooting ideal case.
33. With L ±10% and C ±5%, evaluate $f_{max}$ at 0.9L/0.95C and $f_{min}$ at 1.1L/1.05C.
34. Usable range ends before parasitic self-resonances and unacceptable loss or phase reversal.
35. For 8 Ω and 2.5 kHz, first-order starting values are L ≈ 509 µH and C ≈ 7.96 µF; real drivers require measured impedance and acoustic response.
