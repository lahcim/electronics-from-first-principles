# Part III — LC and Transistor Oscillators

## Lesson 21 — LC Resonance as an Oscillator Core

Review energy exchange between L and C:

$$f_0=\frac1{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}$$

Add loss and an active device that replenishes only the lost energy.

**Challenge:** choose L and C for 1 MHz and discuss realistic Q.

---

## Lesson 22 — Colpitts Oscillator

Use a capacitive divider for feedback. Study feedback ratio, transistor bias, loading, startup, and oscillation frequency based on series-equivalent capacitance.

**Challenge:** design a 1 MHz Colpitts and verify startup margin.

---

## Lesson 23 — Hartley Oscillator

Use an inductive divider or tapped coil. Compare with Colpitts and examine mutual coupling.

**Challenge:** choose L1, L2, and C for a target frequency and feedback ratio.

---

## Lesson 24 — Clapp Oscillator

Add a series capacitor that dominates resonant frequency, reducing sensitivity to transistor and divider capacitances.

**Challenge:** compare frequency pulling between Colpitts and Clapp versions.

---

## Lesson 25 — Transformer-Feedback Oscillators

Study Armstrong-style feedback, dot polarity, turns ratio, core limits, and startup. Reverse winding orientation and observe feedback changing from positive to negative.

**Challenge:** identify correct dot orientation from simulated phase.
