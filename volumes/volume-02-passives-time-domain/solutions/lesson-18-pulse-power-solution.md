# Lesson 18 Challenge — Worked Solution

For 48 V across 22 Ω:

$$I_{PK}=48/22=2.18\text{ A}$$

$$P_{PK}=V^2/R=104.7\text{ W}$$

For a 20 ms pulse:

$$E=P_{PK}t=2.09\text{ J}$$

At one pulse every 10 s:

$$P_{AVG}=E/10=0.209\text{ W}$$

With 2× energy margin, require the resistor and assembly to tolerate at least 4.2 J for a 20 ms pulse at the relevant ambient and initial temperature.

The datasheet review must include pulse-overload or single-pulse energy curves, maximum working voltage, repetitive-pulse derating, continuous power, temperature derating, construction type, and failure mode. A resistor rated only from the 0.209 W average value is not acceptable.
