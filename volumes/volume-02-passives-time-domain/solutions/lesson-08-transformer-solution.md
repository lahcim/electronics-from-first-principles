# Lesson 8 Challenge — Worked Solution

Required reflected impedance:

$$200=\left(\frac{N_1}{N_2}\right)^2(8)$$

Therefore:

$$\frac{N_1}{N_2}=\sqrt{25}=5$$

Use a 5:1 primary-to-secondary turns ratio.

Representative inductances may be $L_1=25\text{ mH}$ and $L_2=1\text{ mH}$ because:

$$\sqrt{L_1/L_2}=5$$

Ideally, secondary voltage is one fifth of primary voltage and secondary current is five times primary current. Real winding resistance reduces output voltage, leakage inductance worsens high-frequency transfer, magnetizing current increases primary current, and core loss/saturation limit voltage-frequency operation.
