# Lesson 25 Challenge — Worked Solution

Given:

- $C_1=100\ \mu F$ charged to 12 V;
- $C_2=220\ \mu F$ initially at 0 V;
- connection resistance 2 Ω.

Final voltage from charge conservation:

$$V_F=\frac{C_1V_1}{C_1+C_2}$$

$$V_F=\frac{100\cdot12}{320}=3.75\text{ V}$$

Initial stored energy:

$$E_i=\frac12(100\ \mu F)(12^2)=7.2\text{ mJ}$$

Final stored energy:

$$E_f=\frac12(320\ \mu F)(3.75^2)=2.25\text{ mJ}$$

Expected dissipated energy:

$$E_{loss}=E_i-E_f=4.95\text{ mJ}$$

Initial current:

$$I_0=\frac{12-0}{2}=6\text{ A}$$

The current decays rapidly. Changing the 2 Ω resistance changes peak current and settling time, but the ideal final voltage and total dissipated energy remain the same.
