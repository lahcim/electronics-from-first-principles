# Lesson 30 Challenge — Worked Solution

Pulse energy:

$$E=Pt=30\cdot0.1=3\text{ J}$$

Average power at one pulse every 2 s:

$$P_{AVG}=3/2=1.5\text{ W}$$

Thermal time constant:

$$\tau_{th}=25\cdot1.2=30\text{ s}$$

For the first 100 ms pulse, cooling is negligible, so temperature rise is approximately:

$$\Delta T\approx E/C_{th}=3/1.2=2.5^\circ C$$

Long-term average rise from average power is approximately:

$$\Delta T_{AVG}\approx1.5\cdot25=37.5^\circ C$$

The cyclic peak will be above this average. The resistor datasheet must still confirm a 30 W, 100 ms pulse is allowed; the thermal RC model does not prove film or element survival.
