# Lesson 5 Design Challenge — Worked Solution

Target: 3.3 mA from 3.3 V.

$$
R=\frac{V}{I}=\frac{3.3\ \text{V}}{3.3\ \text{mA}}=1\ \text{k}\Omega
$$

Conductance:

$$
G=\frac{1}{R}=1\ \text{mS}
$$

At 3.3 V:

$$
I=GV=(1\ \text{mS})(3.3\ \text{V})=3.3\ \text{mA}
$$

Power:

$$
P=VI=(3.3)(3.3\ \text{mA})=10.89\ \text{mW}
$$

A 0.125 W or 0.25 W resistor easily satisfies the 50% rule.

Expected points:

| Voltage | Current |
|---:|---:|
| 0 V | 0 mA |
| 1.65 V | 1.65 mA |
| 3.3 V | 3.3 mA |

A 1 kΩ standard resistor is the reference answer.
