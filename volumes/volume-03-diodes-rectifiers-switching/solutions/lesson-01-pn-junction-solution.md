# Lesson 1 Challenge — Worked Solution

For a tenfold current increase:

$$\frac{I_2}{I_1}=e^{\Delta V/(nV_T)}=10$$

Therefore:

$$\Delta V=nV_T\ln 10$$

At room temperature with $n=2$:

$$\Delta V\approx2(25.9\text{ mV})(2.303)\approx119\text{ mV}$$

So current rises from about 1 mA to 10 mA for roughly 0.12 V additional forward voltage in the ideal exponential region.

At higher current, series resistance, self-heating, high-level injection, and package limits cause the real curve to deviate from the simple Shockley equation.
